Love's Philosophy
The fountains
                         mingle
                                       with the river
And the rivers with the ocean,
The winds of heaven
                                    mix for ever
With a sweet emotion;
Nothing in the world is
                                         single;
All things by a law divine
In one
            spirit
                       meet and mingle.
Why not
                 I with thine?
See the
             mountains
                                 kiss high heaven
And the waves
                          clasp
                                    one another;
No sister-flower would be
                                              forgiven
If it disdained its brother;
And the
               sunlight
                               clasps the earth
And the
               moonbeams
                                    kiss the sea:
What is all this sweet work worth
If thou
              kiss
                     not me?
